Help Reduce Waste With WALL-E

Coming into college with almost no programming or designing experience, one of my first engineering classes required me to step up to the plate, with the professor asking me to work with three of my fellow classmates to engineer a display of sustainability to be shown off at the Boston Museum of Science at the end of the semester. Using tools such as AutoCAD, SolidWorks, RFID, and Arduino, and languages like C and MATLAB, me and my group were able to create a game with a theme of WALL-E where users would aim to recycle RFID scannable objects (cans, bottle, etc.) to make new objects. As the levels would progress, it became more and more difficult to recycle, with the game increasing the difficulty so it would be virtually impossible to succeed towards the end. This design was on purpose to teach the users of the exhibit that it was impossible to recycle all the time, and instead we should reuse our waste. This exhibit not only scored my group and I an A, but was also a huge success at the Museum of Science, teaching the visitors that reusing is a much better option than recycling for long term sustainability.

Duration: Sept 2019 ~ Dec 2019
